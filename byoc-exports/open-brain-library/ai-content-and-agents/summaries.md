### AI doesn't automatically reduce agency — it depends on how you use it

**Source:** Research synthesis  
**Captured:** 2026-03-27

*Tags:* AI integration, agency, cognitive sovereignty

Research note: deep integration (co-authoring with AI) can strengthen control; passive delegation risks 'drift' and erodes autonomy. Co-authors who actively guide AI maintain higher agency than outsourcers who delegate without engagement. Three practices to preserve agency: stay inside the cognitive process, maintain human accountability, recognize warning signs (decision paralysis, skill erosion, emotional dependency).

### Signs of AI overuse: cognitive, emotional, professional

**Source:** Research synthesis  
**Captured:** 2026-03-27

*Tags:* AI overuse, cognitive signs, emotional signs

Cognitive: mental fog, skill erosion, decision paralysis. Emotional: dependency on AI for validation, anxiety when offline, reality blurring. Professional: impersonal outputs, deskilling, accountability gaps. Key insight: risk isn't usage volume but whether you're actively engaged. Co-authors maintain control; outsourcers experience the negative effects.

### How AI affects self-awareness and metacognition

**Source:** Research synthesis  
**Captured:** 2026-03-27

*Tags:* AI, self-awareness, metacognition

AI as reflective tool: mirrors your thought process when co-authoring, amplifies metacognition, highlights skill gaps.
